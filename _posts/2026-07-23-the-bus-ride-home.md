"Tribune Chowk Chandigarh wale!!"

The engine roared as a brazen voice cut through the dreams of the 70-odd passengers in the bus, reminded that they were travelling in a world much more dismal and lacking in sheen compared to where they had been a moment before. As the bus heaved and swayed at each round-about, feeling the curves of the City Beautiful fast asleep, black figures moved within the cabin, stretching, yawning and clearly annoyed at having to face this dismal life of realities. Nevertheless, those shadowy silhouettes proceeded to wake up, stretching their hands just short of the Heavens. Out of the window the flashing city lights from the street lamps illuminated their sleepy faces--dirty, exhausted and hungry. Out in the front a couple of other figures were moving around as well, preparing to get some exercise after their journey, however long or short it might have been.

Just when the chargers were being sought back from neighbours and being stuffed into the chronically obese bags, a flash of light blinded them for A moment, and their ears felt violated by the hoarse voice of the conductor,

 "Chandigarh-wale ready ho jao!!"

"Chandigarh-wale" indeed. The bus had lit up, as if celebrating their prospective departure from it's cabin. After all, these guys had leeched on to her electric supply and blasting AC for so long--the sooner they go, more new passengers can breathe in the dark, sleepy air. With a final turn, the bus proceeded to slow down, until what was left was a low hum of the once-majestic engine. Coming down from the stairs, with heavy bags on their backs, our heroes were greeted by a lonely street, lights glowing white and the roads sounding pale. The conductor got down with remarkable speed and proceeded to get haul the luggage from the cargo area, as eager as the bus to let off these older passengers to make way for the new. A couple started re-arranging the contents of their suitcase, fishing out a coat to cover themselves from the heat in the middle of the night. As the bus rolled away into the distance and disappeared into the horizon with a lurching turn, a nocturnal auto-rickshaw searching for his next (economic) prey approached our vulnerable group. However after countless refusals and a rather exquisite show of defiance, the auto-rickshaw trudged on, disappointed at not getting to score so late in the night. After a while of supposed aimless standing, a large white car pulled up near them.

"Destination kaha hain?"

One OTP later the car glided off into the deserted road. As the turns became more and more familiar and a faint arch of light became visible from afar, our heroes sighed a little. This was not your normal I-have-no-purpose-in-this-world sigh, but rather one tinged with nostalgia, exaustion, hunger, excitement and disappointment. The car came to a halt, and standing in front of the arch, one question burned into the mind of one as his lips let out a small smile:

"Chandigarh wale indeed. But for how long?"
